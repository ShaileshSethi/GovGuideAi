# User Flow

The application should have a simple, intuitive flow that allows users to get answers within a few clicks.

---

## Step 1 – Landing Page

The homepage should include:

- Logo
- Project title
- Tagline
- Large AI search box
- Search button
- Example prompts

Example prompts:

- I lost my Aadhaar card
- I need a passport
- I got married
- My child was born
- I want a driving licence

---

## Step 2 – User Query

The user types their problem in natural language.

Examples:

- I lost my wallet
- I want to study abroad
- I changed my address
- I need a birth certificate

The query is sent to the AI.

---

## Step 3 – AI Analysis

The AI should:

- Understand the user's intent.
- Identify one or more government services.
- Retrieve the required information from the knowledge base.
- Generate a structured response.

---

## Step 4 – Results Page

Display a clear summary at the top.

Example:

**"To apply for a passport, you need 4 documents. The process usually takes 2–3 weeks."**

Below the summary, display one or more Service Cards.

---

## Step 5 – Service Cards

Each card should display:

- Service Name
- Short Description
- Required Documents
- Fees
- Processing Time
- Official Apply Link
- Expand button for more details

---

## Step 6 – Document Checklist

Each service includes a checklist of required documents.

Example:

☐ Aadhaar Card

☐ PAN Card

☐ Birth Certificate

☐ Passport Photo

Users can tick items manually.

---

## Step 7 – Document Details

Clicking a document opens a small popup or expandable section showing:

- Why it is required
- Where to obtain it
- Issuing authority
- Official website

---

## Step 8 – Next Steps

At the bottom of the page, provide:

- Official application link
- Helpful tips
- Common mistakes to avoid
- Suggested related searches

# Design Goal

The entire journey should feel fast, clean, and easy to understand, allowing users to go from a problem to a complete government action plan in just a few minutes.
